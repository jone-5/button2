The Creative Process

Creativity is more than just splattering paint on a blank canvas. It's taking a different route home from work. It's eating a meal with your nondominant hand. It's turning a fimiliar painting upside down. It's watching a movie you've seen 20 times over with the sound off. It's parting your hair on the other side. For me being creative starts with breaking down pardigms. It's all about changing the way you currently see the world. The next time you see something that strikes you as creative, think about how someone must've seen the world differntly to create that.

Reviewing Music

Reviewing music is an opportunity for music lovers to take a deeper dive into the content they love. It invites thought and examination and many times even cross reference. Like any piece of art or literature, music was meant to be explorer and critiqued.

If you want to learn more about reviewing music, Genius Lyrics is a great place to start.

Reviewing an album is not only fun, but it's good writing practice!

Questions to ask when reviewing an album:

Is the album cohesive?
Having a consistent theme can greatly increase the quality of an album and leave a distinct impression on the listener.
Does the album have a message?
Music is not only a form of art but also communication. What does the album communicate if anything?

Steps for Reviewing an Album

  1. Do some background research on the artist. Where does this album fall in their discography? Have they traditionally mademusic in this same genre?
  2. Listen to the selected album all the way through in the order it was intended to be listened to.
  3. Pay special attention to themes that show up more than one time throughout the album.
  4. Take note of the following as you listen a second time:
    • musical tone
    • shifts in tone
    • lyrical clues
  5. Write your review including your findings and opinions!
